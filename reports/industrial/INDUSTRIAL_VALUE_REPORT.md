# Preliminary Industrial Value Audit

The operational concern is whether equal expected capacity slack implies equal
collapse exposure when the disruption-demand composition differs. A negative
answer would warn practitioners not to calibrate buffers from drift alone.

The smoke experiment measures collapse probability and time to collapse. It
does not yet measure policy cost, service level, or intervention benefit, so its
industrial evidence is diagnostic rather than decision-ready.

Existing local work already studies critical boundaries, threshold policies,
network-aware control, and digital twins. Any future publication must add a
decision variable, operational baseline, uncertainty analysis, and external
novelty verification rather than present this diagnostic as a contribution.
