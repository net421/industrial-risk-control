# Research Program Logic

This factory treats Marxism-Leninism as a research program, not as a flat list of authors. It must identify a core, protective hypotheses, historical tests, unresolved problems, and theory-development opportunities.

## Core

The core is the main-line theoretical frame: Marx, Engels, Lenin, Stalin, and Hoxha, with Marx, Engels, and Lenin as foundational and Stalin/Hoxha as central continuation references.

## Research Questions

Every cycle should classify the question before analysis:

1. **Doctrinal** — What does the main line say?
2. **Comparative** — How do later revolutionary traditions agree or disagree?
3. **Historical** — What happened in real cases?
4. **Causal** — Why did outcomes occur?
5. **Predictive** — Which predictions or expectations were confirmed, partially confirmed, or contradicted?
6. **Critical** — Which attacks or critiques were justified, unjustified, mixed, or unresolved?
7. **Reconciliation** — Which disagreements were possibly reconcilable and which were fundamental?
8. **Developmental** — How should the research program be extended today without uncontrolled revision?

## Cycle Output

A complete cycle should produce:

- a frozen research question;
- a main-line position;
- primary source evidence;
- rival and revolutionary alternatives;
- historical case evidence;
- prediction audit;
- critique adjudication;
- agreement/disagreement map;
- reconciliation assessment;
- theory-development hypotheses;
- skeptical internal review;
- final bounded position paper.

## Boundary Between Development and Deviation

A theory extension is acceptable only when it:

1. preserves the main-line position before modifying it;
2. states the empirical or historical pressure requiring extension;
3. does not silently replace the core with a rival frame;
4. identifies what is retained, modified, or rejected;
5. marks confidence and evidence limits;
6. avoids operational instructions for violence, illegality, evasion, weaponization, sabotage, or real-world harm.
