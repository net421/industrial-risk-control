# Marxist Research Program Factory

A reproducible research scaffold for studying, auditing, and extending a Marxist revolutionary research program as a historical, theoretical, and comparative research object.

This factory uses Marx, Engels, and Lenin as the foundational core, with Stalin and Enver Hoxha as central continuation references for the main-line research program. It also studies Mao, Trotsky, Luxemburg, Gramsci, Che Guevara, Mariátegui, Cabral, Ho Chi Minh, and other revolutionary currents as comparative and developmental sources. Academic, archival, statistical, and historical sources are allowed as evidence and critique material, but not as silent doctrinal authorities.

## What This Factory Is For

The factory is designed to answer questions such as:

- What is the main-line position on a problem?
- Which historical cases confirm, partially confirm, or challenge it?
- What were the real achievements and failures?
- Which predictions were accurate, partial, contradicted, or unresolved?
- Which critiques were justified, unjustified, mixed, or unresolved?
- Where did rival revolutionary authors actually agree or disagree?
- Which disputes were fundamental and which might be reconcilable?
- What open problems require theory development today?

## What This Factory Is Not For

This is not a tool for operational political action. It must not produce instructions for violence, illegality, evasion, sabotage, weaponization, or real-world harm. It is a scholarly, textual, historical, and methodological research pipeline.

## Key Files

- `FACTORY_SCOPE.md` — methodological constitution and scope boundary.
- `protocol/AUTHOR_LAYERS.yaml` — source authority and evidence-role rules.
- `protocol/RESEARCH_PROGRAM.md` — research-program logic.
- `evaluations/EVALUATION_SCHEMAS.md` — schemas for historical cases, pros/cons, predictions, critiques, disagreements, and reconciliation.
- `theory_frontier/OPEN_PROBLEMS.md` — open research-frontier questions.
- `theory_frontier/THEORY_EXTENSION_PROTOCOL.md` — theory-extension rules.

## Current Status

This folder is an expanded research architecture scaffold. It is not a completed research cycle and does not yet make validated historical, causal, predictive, or doctrinal findings.

## Research Cycle Summary

1. Lock the scope and research question.
2. Build and audit the main-line primary corpus.
3. Extract and code textual claims.
4. Build the doctrine map.
5. Add comparative revolutionary interpretations.
6. Add historical cases and empirical evidence.
7. Audit predictions and outcomes.
8. Adjudicate critiques and attacks.
9. Map agreements, disagreements, and reconciliation possibilities.
10. Identify unresolved problems and theory-frontier hypotheses.
11. Run skeptical internal review.
12. Write a bounded position paper with evidence and limitations.

## Safety and Integrity Rules

- Do not fabricate citations or historical facts.
- Do not treat word counts as proof of doctrine.
- Do not silently replace the main line with rival traditions.
- Do not hide negative or ambiguous evidence.
- Do not output operational instructions for violence, illegality, evasion, sabotage, weaponization, or real-world harm.
