# Theory Extension Protocol

The factory may develop theory, but only through a controlled procedure.

## Step 1 — Preserve the Original Position

State the main-line position first. Include primary textual evidence and identify whether the position comes from Marx, Engels, Lenin, Stalin, Hoxha, or another central source.

## Step 2 — Identify the Pressure

Classify the reason an extension is being considered:

- new historical evidence;
- failed prediction;
- partial prediction;
- implementation failure;
- unresolved contradiction;
- rival revolutionary insight;
- changed material conditions;
- academic or empirical evidence;
- hostile critique that survives adjudication.

## Step 3 — Classify the Challenge

Use one of these labels:

- misunderstanding;
- implementation error;
- unsupported hostile critique;
- historically bounded limitation;
- unresolved contradiction;
- compatible extension;
- incompatible deviation.

## Step 4 — Propose a Hypothesis

A theory-development hypothesis must include:

- the retained core claim;
- the modified or extended claim;
- the evidence requiring extension;
- expected observable implications;
- conditions under which the hypothesis would fail;
- relation to rival interpretations;
- confidence level.

## Step 5 — Skeptical Review

Before entering the final report, the hypothesis must pass skeptical review:

- Is there primary evidence for the original position?
- Is the historical evidence real and not cherry-picked?
- Is this extension actually compatible with the main line?
- Does it silently import a rival framework?
- Does it confuse doctrine, history, causality, prediction, and normative judgment?
- Does it avoid operational instructions for violence, illegality, evasion, weaponization, sabotage, or real-world harm?
